## Part 2: Causality (What Influences What, and When)

### Standard Relativistic Causality vs. Absolute-Time Causality

**In standard relativity:**
- **Light cone structure** defines causality: event A can influence event B only if B is in A's future light cone
- Spacelike-separated events are **causally disconnected** (no faster-than-light influence)
- This is **local causality**

**In architrino theory (absolute time + finite $c_f$):**
- **Absolute simultaneity** exists: events simultaneous in absolute time $t$ are objectively simultaneous
- **Potential propagation** at $c_f$ defines a **causal influence structure**, but:
  - Architrinos at spacelike separation (by the $c_f$ metric) can still have **correlated states** because they share the same absolute time
  - Their states at $t$ were set by their **entire past history** in absolute time, which may include overlapping causal pasts

**Key distinction:**
- **No faster-than-light signaling**: An architrino at position A cannot **send a controllable signal** to position B faster than $c_f$
- **But**: Correlations can exist between spacelike-separated architrinos if their **past light cones overlap** at earlier absolute times

### Acausal Influences? Or Just Non-Local Correlations?

**The outer binary scenario** (transition outcome depends on spacelike-separated architrinos):

**Option 1: Retrocausality (I don't think this is happening)**
- The future state of the binary **causes** the past emission from distant architrinos
- This would require closed causal loops in absolute time
- **Rejected**: The theory has a strict arrow of absolute time; no backwards causation

**Option 2: Non-Local Correlations (Bell-Style)**
- The binary's transition and the distant architrino's state are **correlated** not because one causes the other, but because:
  - They share a **common causal past** (at some earlier absolute time, potentials from a shared source reached both)
  - The correlation was **set up** when their past light cones overlapped
  - At the moment of measurement/transition, we're **reading out** that pre-existing correlation

This is exactly **Bell nonlocality**: spacelike-separated events exhibit correlations stronger than local hidden-variable theories allow, but:
- **No faster-than-light signaling** (you can't control the outcome to send a message)
- **Deterministic** at the level of the complete wavefunction (or, here, complete architrino configuration)
- **Appears random** to observers who don't have access to the complete state

**The self-hit wrinkle ($v > c_f$ sources):**

Architrinos moving faster than $c_f$ are in **self-hit regime**:
- They interact with their **own past potential wake**
- This introduces **non-Markovian memory**: the future trajectory depends on the entire past history, not just the current state
- If such an architrino is **outside the causal past** of the binary (by the $c_f$ metric), its emitted potential may still **reach** the binary because:
  - The potential was emitted from a **past location** that **was** in causal contact
  - The self-hit dynamics modified the emission pattern in a history-dependent way

**Implication:**
- The binary's transition can depend on the **path history** of distant superluminal architrinos
- This is **not retrocausation** (past affects future, as usual)
- But it **looks acausal** from the effective quantum description because the "hidden variable" (superluminal architrino path) is inaccessible and chaotic

### Chaos vs. Randomness: A Crucial Distinction

**Chaos** (deterministic):
- Exponential sensitivity to initial conditions
- **Predictable in principle** (given exact initial state)
- **Unpredictable in practice** (because we can never know initial state exactly, and errors grow exponentially)
- **Example**: Double pendulum, weather, turbulence

**Randomness** (stochastic):
- Irreducible unpredictability
- **Unpredictable even in principle** (no hidden state determines the outcome)
- **Example** (in standard QM): Radioactive decay, photon detection at a beamsplitter

**The architrino theory (under Option 1) claims:**
- What **looks like** quantum randomness is actually **deterministic chaos**
- The "random" outcomes of measurements are **chaotic amplifications** of microscopic architrino configurations
- There's a **hidden variable theory** underneath, but the variables are so complex and sensitive that the system is **effectively indistinguishable** from true randomness

**Testability:**
- If this is right, we might **never** be able to predict individual outcomes (chaos prevents it)
- But we should be able to show that the **statistics** (Born rule, entanglement correlations) emerge from chaotic dynamics
- This is a **theoretical challenge**: derive Born rule from master equation
